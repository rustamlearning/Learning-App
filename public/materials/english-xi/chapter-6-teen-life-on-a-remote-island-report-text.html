2. Situational Hook

Pulau Sapuka adalah pulau kecil. Di tempat seperti ini, hampir semua orang saling mengenal. Keluarga, tetangga, teman sekolah, dan masyarakat sering terhubung erat. Hal ini bisa menjadi kekuatan karena orang-orang saling membantu. Namun, kehidupan di pulau kecil juga memiliki tekanan tersendiri bagi remaja.

Sebagian remaja hidup di antara banyak harapan. Mereka diharapkan rajin sekolah, membantu orang tua, menjaga nama baik keluarga, menghormati nilai-nilai masyarakat, dan tetap memikirkan masa depan. Di sisi lain, akses internet yang semakin mudah membuat remaja terhubung dengan dunia luar. Mereka melihat banyak gaya hidup baru melalui media sosial, game online, video pendek, dan percakapan digital.

Dalam chapter ini, kita tidak akan menghakimi remaja. Kita akan belajar melihat kehidupan remaja sebagai sebuah fenomena sosial. Kita akan menggunakan Report Text untuk melaporkan fakta secara netral, sopan, dan berdasarkan pengamatan atau survei sederhana.

6. MoT — Modelling of the Text

Main Model Text

Teen Life on a Remote Island: Challenges and Choices

General Classification

Teenagers on remote islands experience adolescence in a unique social environment. They grow up in small communities where family, school, friendship, local values, and natural conditions are closely connected. In places such as Sapuka, teenage life is shaped by island geography, strong community relationships, limited opportunities, and increasing internet access. These factors influence how young people study, communicate, build friendships, make choices, and plan their future.

Description

Social Expectations

In small island communities, teenagers are often known by many people around them. Their actions can be seen not only as personal choices but also as part of family and community life. Because of this, many teenagers are expected to behave respectfully, help their parents, follow school rules, and protect the good name of their family. These expectations can guide teenagers to become responsible. However, they can also create pressure when teenagers feel that they must always meet other people’s expectations.

Family and Gender Roles

Family has an important role in teenage life. Many students support their families by helping with household work, fishing-related activities, seaweed farming, or younger siblings. In some families, boys and girls may receive different expectations. Boys may be encouraged to work outside or help at sea, while girls may be expected to help more at home. These roles are part of community habits, but they may also affect students’ study time, confidence, and future planning. Teenagers need support so that both boys and girls can continue learning and developing their abilities.

Internet and Social Media

Internet access is changing teenage life on remote islands. With better connection, students can find learning materials, watch educational videos, communicate with teachers, and learn about the wider world. At the same time, young people are also exposed to online games, short videos, social media trends, and digital comparison. Some teenagers are influenced by what they see online. Their study habits, sleep time, self-confidence, and social relationships can be affected by excessive screen time. Therefore, digital self-control is an important skill for teenagers.

Peer Pressure and Relationships

Friendship is very important during adolescence. Friends can motivate students to study, join positive activities, and stay confident. However, peer pressure can also influence teenagers’ choices. Some teenagers may follow their friends even when the activity is not good for their learning or future. Relationship boundaries are also important. Healthy friendship and respectful relationships can support teenagers’ growth, while unhealthy pressure can reduce focus, motivation, and emotional safety. Schools and families can help teenagers understand responsibility, respect, and safe boundaries.

Education and Future Choices

Education is one of the most important ways for teenagers to prepare for the future. In remote island areas, students may face challenges such as limited facilities, delayed sea transportation, family responsibilities, and unstable motivation. Some teenagers may think about working early or stopping school because of personal or family situations. These issues should be discussed carefully and respectfully. Teenagers need guidance, encouragement, and realistic future planning. They also need to understand that small daily choices can influence their long-term dreams.

Community Strengths

Although teenagers on remote islands face many challenges, they also have strong support systems. Island communities often have close family relationships, helpful neighbors, religious values, local wisdom, and strong solidarity. When students face problems, support from teachers, parents, friends, and community members can help them make better choices. A strong community can protect teenagers by giving advice, emotional support, learning motivation, and positive examples.

Closing Statement

Teen life on a remote island is shaped by challenges and strengths. Social expectations, family roles, internet access, peer pressure, and future choices influence teenagers in different ways. A factual report helps people understand these issues without judging young people. It also reminds the community that teenagers need guidance, respect, support, and opportunities to protect their future.

A. Simple Present Tense for Report Text

Function: Dalam Report Text, simple present tense digunakan untuk menjelaskan fakta umum, kebiasaan, karakteristik, atau fenomena yang masih berlaku.

Contoh Bahasa Indonesia: Remaja di pulau kecil menghadapi tekanan sosial. Internet memengaruhi kebiasaan belajar siswa. Komunitas pulau memiliki solidaritas yang kuat.

In English: Teenagers on small islands face social pressure. The internet influences students’ study habits. Island communities have strong solidarity.

Pattern 1: Subject + Verb 1 / Verb-s/es

SubjectVerbObject/Complement
Many teenagersusethe internet after school.
A small communityshapesteenagers’ behavior.
Social mediainfluencesstudents’ confidence.
Some studentshelptheir parents after school.

Catatan: Gunakan verb-s/es jika subjeknya singular: A student uses, Social media influences, A small community shapes. Gunakan verb 1 jika subjeknya plural: Students use, Teenagers face, Families support.

B. Passive Voice in Report Text

Function: Passive voice digunakan ketika kita ingin fokus pada fenomena atau dampak, bukan pada siapa yang melakukan tindakan. Dalam Report Text, passive voice membuat bahasa terdengar lebih formal, netral, dan tidak menyalahkan individu.

Active: Peer pressure influences teenagers.
Passive: Teenagers are influenced by peer pressure.

Model Text A — Easy Level: Teenagers and Internet Access

General Classification

Internet access is an important part of teenage life today. In many places, including remote islands, teenagers use the internet for learning, communication, and entertainment.

Description

In Sapuka, internet access helps students find information for school assignments. Students can watch learning videos, search for English words, and communicate with teachers or classmates. Internet access can make learning easier, especially when books and other learning materials are limited.

However, internet access also brings challenges. Some teenagers spend too much time watching short videos, playing online games, or scrolling social media. These habits may reduce study time and sleep time. Teenagers need digital self-control so they can use the internet in a healthy way.

Internet access is not only a tool for entertainment. It can also support education, creativity, and future planning. With good guidance, teenagers can use the internet to learn new skills and protect their dreams.

Model Text B — Medium Level: Peer Pressure in Small Communities

General Classification

Peer pressure is a social influence that comes from friends or people of the same age. It is common during adolescence because teenagers often want to be accepted by their group.

Description

In small communities, peer pressure can feel stronger because many teenagers know each other closely. Students may spend time with the same friends at school, near their homes, at the harbor, or around the village. Positive peer pressure can encourage students to study, join school activities, attend class regularly, and speak politely.

However, peer pressure can also lead teenagers to make choices that are not helpful for their future. Some students may follow friends who skip class, ignore assignments, stay up too late, or spend too much time online. These choices may affect school attendance, motivation, and family trust.

Peer pressure is not always negative. It depends on the group’s values and habits. When friends support learning, respect, and responsibility, peer pressure can become a positive force. Teenagers need to choose friends carefully and build healthy friendship boundaries.

Questions

  1. What is peer pressure?
  2. Why can peer pressure feel stronger in small communities?
  3. Give two examples of positive peer pressure.
  4. Give two examples of negative peer pressure.
  5. How can teenagers manage peer pressure?

Fact vs Opinion: “Peer pressure is common during adolescence.” / “Students who follow friends are always weak.”

Reflection: What kind of friend can help you protect your dream?

Model Text C — Local Context: Youth Life in Sapuka

General Classification

Youth life in Sapuka is shaped by island geography, family life, school, community values, and internet access. Teenagers in Sapuka grow up in a small island environment where the sea, family responsibilities, friendship, and education are closely connected.

Description

Many students in Sapuka live near the sea. Some students help their families with fishing activities, seaweed farming, or household responsibilities. These activities teach responsibility and discipline. At the same time, they may reduce students’ study time when students do not manage their schedule well.

School plays an important role in youth development. Teachers help students learn English, science, social values, and future skills. However, students may face challenges such as low motivation, irregular attendance, or limited learning facilities. Weather and sea transportation can also affect school activities in island areas.

Internet access has changed youth life in Sapuka. Students can search for information, communicate more easily, and learn from digital content. However, social media and online entertainment can also influence teenagers’ habits. Some students may compare themselves with people online or spend too much time on their phones.

Community life is one of Sapuka’s strengths. Families, neighbors, teachers, and local leaders can become a support system for teenagers. When young people receive guidance and respect, they can make better choices and prepare for their future.

Questions

Model Text D — Issue-Based: Social Expectations and Future Choices

General Classification

Social expectations are beliefs about how people should behave in a community. During adolescence, these expectations can influence teenagers’ choices, confidence, relationships, education, and future planning.

Description

In small island communities, social expectations are often connected to family reputation, respect for elders, local traditions, and community values. Teenagers are expected to behave politely, help their families, attend school, and avoid actions that may create social problems. These expectations can guide young people to become responsible members of the community.

However, social expectations can also create pressure. Some teenagers may feel that their choices are watched by many people. Others may feel confused when their personal dreams are different from what people expect. For example, a student may want to continue studying outside the island, but the family may need help at home. Another student may want to focus on school, but peer groups may invite the student to spend more time outside class.

Gender roles may also influence future choices. Boys and girls sometimes receive different expectations about work, study, relationships, or family responsibilities. These expectations should be discussed respectfully. Both boys and girls need equal encouragement to continue learning, protect their health, and plan their future.

Social expectations are not always negative. They can protect teenagers when they promote respect, responsibility, and care. The challenge is to balance community values with students’ dreams. Teenagers need guidance from family, teachers, and community members so they can make responsible choices without losing their hope for the future.

Questions

  1. What are social expectations?
  2. What are teenagers expected to do in small island communities?
  3. Why can social expectations create pressure?

Mr. Arman: Good morning, Rina. I am happy to join.

Rina: What kind of pressure do teenagers usually face?

Mr. Arman: Some teenagers face family expectations, peer pressure, and questions about their future. Some students also feel pressure from social media. They compare their lives with what they see online.

Rina: Does internet access bring positive effects for students?

Mr. Arman: Yes, of course. Internet access helps students find learning materials, watch educational videos, and communicate with teachers. It opens a wider world for students in remote areas.

Rina: What are the challenges of internet access?

Mr. Arman: The challenge is self-control. Some students spend too much time playing online games, watching short videos, or scrolling social media. This can affect sleep time, study focus, and motivation.

Rina: Some teenagers also face pressure in friendship or relationships. How should we talk about this issue?

Mr. Arman: We should talk about it respectfully. Teenagers are still learning to make decisions. They need guidance, not shame. Healthy friendship and relationship boundaries are important. Students need to protect their education, emotional safety, and future dreams.

Rina: What role can school and family play?

Mr. Arman: School and family can become a strong support system. Teachers can guide students. Parents can listen and encourage them. Friends can support positive habits. Community members can also give good examples.

Rina: What message do you want to give to teenagers in Sapuka?

Mr. Arman: My message is simple: your island may be small, but your future can be wide. Manage your choices, respect your family, choose good friends, use the internet wisely, and protect your dreams.

Rina: Thank you, Sir. That is a meaningful message.

Mr. Arman: You are welcome.

Dialogue 2 — Writing Without Judging

Student: Sir, I want to write, “Students who date too much are bad.” Is that okay?

Teacher: That sentence sounds judgmental. In a report text, we need neutral language.

Student: How can I make it better?

Teacher: You can write, “Some students may lose focus when they do not set healthy relationship boundaries.”

Student: So, I should focus on the issue, not attack the person?

Teacher: Exactly. Report text explains a phenomenon. It does not shame people.

Student: What if the topic is sensitive?

Teacher: Use respectful language. Focus on responsibility, support, education, and future planning.

Student: I understand. A good report should be factual and fair.

Teacher: Excellent.
